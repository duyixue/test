Heston Model Volatility Estimation Problem¶

1. Brief Introduction¶

As we know, volatility plays a critical role in derivatives pricing, risk management and portfolio optimization. One of the compelling approaches to estimate the volatility is the stochastic model. In this research, we try to leverage Heston model to estimate the volatility based on SPX data. By referring to a few academic papers, we first developed computational methods to integrated out the historical volatility in the formula of likelihood function, and then implement the implied volatility into the Heston model. The estimated results are tested in the real market data.

2. Methodology¶

  • Summary of Two Methods

2.1 Methodology #1¶

(Atiya & Wall, 2009)

2.1.1 Data Source¶

SPX data from Yahoo Finance.

2.1.2 The Heston Model¶

$s_t$ represents the asset price at time $t$, $v_t$ represents the variance of log_price, if it follows the Heston model, $s_t$ and $v_t$ follow the following SDE:

$$ds_t=\mu s_tdt+\sqrt{v_t}s_tdW_t$$$$dv_t=(\alpha-\beta v_t)dt+\sigma \sqrt{v_t}dZ_t$$$$E(dW_tdZ_t) = \rho dt$$

2.1.3 Parameters¶

Parameters of the Heston model to fit are $(\mu, \alpha, \beta, \sigma, \rho)$.

  • $y_t=ln(s_t)$ is the log price, then it follows a normal distribution
  • $\Delta y_{t+1}=y_{t+1}-y_t$ is the log return at time $t+1$
  • $\Delta$ = Size of time step
  • $\mu$ = Drift term
  • $\alpha/\beta$ = Long-run mean
  • $\beta$ = Speed of reverting to the mean
  • $\sigma$ = Volatility of the volatility
  • $\rho=corr(dW_t,dZ_t)$

2.1.4 The Recursive Algorithm¶

  • S1: Starting at $t=0$ with a uniform grid for volatility $v_0$, choose a prior distribution for $v_0$: exponential, log-normal, chi-squared

  • S2: Obtain $v_{t+1} = \sqrt{B^2-C}-B$, where

$$B=-\alpha\Delta-\rho\sigma(\Delta y_{t+1}-\mu\Delta)$$$$C=\alpha^2\Delta^2+2\rho\sigma\alpha\Delta(\Delta y_{t+1}-\mu\Delta)+\sigma^2(\Delta y_{t+1}-\mu\Delta)^2-2v_t^2a\sigma^2(1-\rho^2)\Delta$$

  • S3: Obtain $L_{t+1} = Constant \times d_t(ab_t)^{-0.25}e^{-2\sqrt{ab_t}}L_t$; Constant = 1e-30 every 50 steps to avoid overflow.

  • S4: If t<T, return to S2; if t=T, go to S5;

  • S5: Maximize the integration of $L_T$ with respect to $v_T$ (approximated by computing Riemann sum), i.e.

$$L_T^{'}=\int_{v_T}L_Tdv_T$$

Exact formulas

2.2 Methodology #2¶

(Ait-Sahalia & Kimmel, 2007)

2.2.1 Data Source¶

SPX and VIX data from Yahoo Finance.

2.2.2 The Heston Model¶

$S_t$ represents the log SPX price at time $t$, $Y_t$ represents the local variance, which is originally unobservable, if it follows the Heston model, $s_t=ln(S_t)$ and $Y_t$ follow the following SDE:

$$d\begin{bmatrix}s_t\\Y_t\end{bmatrix}=\begin{bmatrix}r-d-\frac{1}{2}Y_t\\ \kappa (\gamma-Y_t)\end{bmatrix}dt+ \begin{bmatrix}\sqrt{(1-\rho^2)Y_t}&\rho \sqrt{Y_t}\\0&\sigma \sqrt{Y_t}\end{bmatrix} d\begin{bmatrix}W_1(t)\\W_2(t)\end{bmatrix}$$

where:

  • $r=$ Risk free rate. We fix it to 4% in the further calculations.
  • $d=$ Dividend yield. We fix it to 1.5% in the further calculations.
  • $\kappa$ = The speed of reverting to the mean
  • $\gamma$ = The long-run mean

2.2.3 Parameters¶

Parameters of the Heston model in this representation are $\theta=(\kappa,\gamma,\sigma, \rho)$. And we will use likelihood function to optimize the parameters $\theta$.

2.2.4 The Recursive Algorithm¶

The Likelihood Function¶

The function is calculated recursively, using the previous step $x$'s information to calculate the $x$ for all time intervals. $$l_X^{(J)}(\Delta,x|x_0;\theta)=-\frac{m}{2}ln(2\pi \Delta)-D_v(x;\theta)+\frac{C_X^{(j_{-1},-1)}(x|x_0;\theta)}{\Delta} +\sum_{k=0}^{J}C_X^{(j_k,k)}(x|x_0;\theta)\frac{\Delta^k}{k!}$$

where:

  • $m=$ Dimension of the risk factors(Here we have Log SPX price and VIX)
  • $C_X=$ Coefficients
  • $D_v(x;\theta)=\frac{1}{2}ln(det[v(x;\theta)])$
  • $\nu(x)=\sigma(x)\sigma^{\prime}(x)$

2.2.5 Proxy for $Y_t$¶

In the paper, the author regards $Y_t$ as latent variable and we can observe option price in the market. We choose VIX index as an good estimation of implied volatility of SPX index.

  • Method 1 $$Y_t\approx \frac{B\times VIX^2\times(T-t)+A\times(T-t)}{e^{B(T-t)}-1}-\frac{A}{B}$$ where:
    • $A=\kappa\gamma$
    • $B=-\kappa$
    • For $VIX$, we set $T-t=\frac{30}{365}$
$$$$
  • Method 2 $$Y_t\approx VIX^2$$

3.1.5 Analysis of the model¶

Possible Reasons about the problems in the algorithm

  • The simulated(real) v_t is not necessary the path with the maximum likelihood,
  • The time interval is not long enough for the calculation.
  • There may exist some problems when the likelihood function is estimated in the paper.
  • The result depends on the parameters we choose in the algorithm. For example, the starting point of v0, the length of time in total, the prior function of v0 and the theta we choose in the algorithm.
