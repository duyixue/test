Heston Model Volatility Estimation Problem¶

1. Brief Introduction¶

As we know, volatility plays a critical role in derivatives pricing, risk management and portfolio optimization. One of the compelling approaches to estimate the volatility is the stochastic model. In this research, we try to leverage Heston model to estimate the volatility based on SPX data. By referring to a few academic papers, we first developed computational methods to integrated out the historical volatility in the formula of likelihood function, and then implement the implied volatility into the Heston model. The estimated results are tested in the real market data.

2. Methodology¶

2.1 Methodology for method 1¶

(Atiya & Wall, 2009)

The Heston Model¶

$s_t$ represents the asset price at time $t$, $v_t$ represents the variance of log_price, if it follows the Heston model, $s_t$ and $v_t$ follow the following SDE:

$$ds_t=\mu s_tdt+\sqrt{v_t}s_tdW_t$$$$dv_t=(\alpha-\beta v_t)dt+\sigma \sqrt{v_t}dZ_t$$$$E(dW_tdZ_t) = \rho dt$$

Parameters¶

Parameters of the Heston model are $(\mu, \alpha, \beta, \sigma, \rho)$.

  • $y_t=log(s_t)$ is the log price, then it follows a normal distribution;

  • $\Delta y_{t+1}=y_{t+1}-y_t$ is the log return at time $t+1$;

  • $\Delta$ is the size of time step;

Other parameters include:

  • $\beta^{'}=1-\beta\Delta$

  • $a=\frac{\beta^2+\rho\sigma\beta^{'}\Delta+\frac{\sigma^2\Delta^2}{4}}{2\sigma^2(1-\rho^2)\Delta}$

  • $D=2\pi\sigma\sqrt{1-\rho^2}\Delta$

Algorithm Introduction: the Recursive Algorithm¶

Parameters¶

  • $b_t=\frac{(v_{t+1}-\alpha\Delta)^2 - 2\rho\sigma(v_{t+1}-\alpha\Delta)(\Delta y_{t+1}-\mu\Delta) + \sigma^2(\Delta y_{t+1}-\mu\Delta)^2}{2\sigma^2(1-\rho^2)\Delta}$

  • $d_t=exp\{\frac{(2\beta^{'}+\rho\sigma\Delta)(v_{t+1}-\alpha\Delta) - (2\rho\sigma\beta^{'}+\sigma^2\Delta)(\Delta y_{t+1}-\mu\Delta)}{2\sigma^2(1-\rho^2)\Delta}\} \div D$

Algorithm¶

  • STEP1: Starting at $t=0$ with a uniform grid for volatility $v_0$, choose a prior distribution for $v_0$: exponential, log-normal, chi-squared

  • STEP2: Obtain $v_{t+1} = \sqrt{B^2-C}-B$, where

$$B=-\alpha\Delta-\rho\sigma(\Delta y_{t+1}-\mu\Delta)$$$$C=\alpha^2\Delta^2+2\rho\sigma\alpha\Delta(\Delta y_{t+1}-\mu\Delta)+\sigma^2(\Delta y_{t+1}-\mu\Delta)^2-2v_t^2a\sigma^2(1-\rho^2)\Delta$$

  • STEP3: Obtain $L_{t+1}\propto d_t(ab_t)^{-0.25}e^{-2\sqrt{ab_t}}L_t$ (formula 28)

  • STEP4: If t<T, return to S2; if t=T, go to S5;

  • STEP5: Maximize the sum of $L_t$ derived by different $v_0$, ie

$$L_T^{'}=\int_{v_T}L_Tdv_T$$

2.2 Methodology for Model2¶

(Ait-Sahalia & Kimmel, 2007)

The Heston Model¶

$S_t$ represents the asset price at time $t$, $Y_t$ represents the local variance, if it follows the Heston model, $s_t=lnS_t$ and $Y_t$ follow the following SDE:

$$d\begin{bmatrix}s_t\\Y_t\end{bmatrix}=\begin{bmatrix}r-d-\frac{1}{2}Y_t\\ \kappa (\gamma-Y_t)\end{bmatrix}dt+ \begin{bmatrix}\sqrt{(1-\rho^2)Y_t}&\rho \sqrt{Y_t}\\0&\sigma \sqrt{Y_t}\end{bmatrix} d\begin{bmatrix}W_1(t)\\W_2(t)\end{bmatrix}$$

Parameters¶

Parameters of the Heston model in this representation are $\theta=(\sigma, \rho, \lambda_1, \lambda_2)$. And we will use likelihood function to optimize the parameters $\theta$.

Other parameters include:

  • $r=$ Risk free rate
  • $d=$ Dividend yield

Algorithm Introduction: the Recursive Algorithm¶

The Likelihood function¶

$$l_X^{(J)}(\Delta,x|x_0;\theta)=-\frac{m}{2}ln(2\pi \Delta)-D_{\nu}(x;\theta)+\frac{C_X^{(j_{-1},-1)}(x|x_0;\theta)}{\Delta} +\sum_{k=0}^{J}C_X^{(j_k,k)}(x|x_0;\theta)\frac{\Delta^k}{k!}$$

The function is calculated recursively, using $x_0$ information to calculate the $x$ for all time intervals.

Parameters¶

  • $D_{\nu}(x;\theta)=\frac{1}{2}ln(det[\nu(x;\theta)])$
  • $\nu(x)=\sigma(x)\sigma^{\prime}(x)$
  • $C_X=$ Call option price
  • $m=$ Dimension of the brownian motion defined

Proxy for $Y_t$¶

  • Method 1 $$Y_t\approx \frac{(bV_{imp}(t,T)+a)(T-t)}{e^b(T-t)-1}-\frac{a}{b}$$ where:
    • $V_{imp}(t,T)$ is the observed implied variance of a short-maturity $T$ at-the-money option
    • $a= \kappa^{'}\gamma^{'}$
    • $b=-\kappa^{'}$
$$$$
  • Method 2 $$Y_t\approx V_{imp}(t,T)$$

                                 1 & \rho\sigma\\
                             \rho\sigma & \sigma^2
                              \end{matrix} \right]$

3.1.5 Analysis of the model¶

Possible Reasons about the problems in the algorithm

  • The simulated(real) v_t is not necessary the path with the maximum likelihood,
  • The time interval is not long enough for the calculation.
  • The result depends on the parameters we choose in the algorithm. For example, the starting point of v0, the length of time in total, the prior function of v0 and the theta we choose in the algorithm.
